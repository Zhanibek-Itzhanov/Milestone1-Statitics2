# Milestone1-Statitics2
Volatility and Market Sensitivity Analysis: BMW & MBG (2025–2026)Project OverviewThis repository provides a comprehensive statistical analysis of the weekly returns for BMW AG (BMW.DE) and Mercedes-Benz Group (MBG.DE) from March 2025 to March 2026. The study evaluates market risk, volatility benchmarking, and asset sensitivity to the broader market index, the S&P 500 (^GSPC).MethodologyThe analysis leverages 52 weeks of historical price data and utilizes the following statistical frameworks:Descriptive Statistics: Calculation of mean returns, sample variance, standard deviation, coefficient of variation, and Sharpe ratios.Chi-Square ($\chi^2$) Goodness-of-Fit Test: Used to assess whether the realized weekly volatility of BMW and MBG significantly exceeds the internal benchmark (derived from a 15% annual standard deviation, where $\sigma_0^2 = 0.0004$).F-Test: A comparative variance analysis to determine if there is a statistically significant difference in the volatility profiles between BMW and MBG.Regression Analysis: Modeling the relationship between the weekly returns of BMW/MBG and the S&P 500 index to measure market sensitivity ($R^2$ and Beta).Key FindingsVolatility Benchmark: The internal target variance was set at 0.0004.Statistical Conclusions:$\chi^2$ Test: The null hypothesis was rejected for both assets, confirming that realized weekly volatility is significantly higher than the 15% annual target.F-Test: No statistically significant difference in variance was found between BMW and MBG.Correlation: A strong positive correlation ($\approx 0.74$) exists between the two automotive stocks.Market Sensitivity: The regression analysis against the S&P 500 shows an $R^2 \approx 0.105$, indicating that market-wide trends account for a portion of the stocks' return behavior, but idiosyncratic risk remains high.Repository Structure/data: Historical price data for BMW, MBG, and the S&P 500 index./prep: Processed arithmetic and geometric weekly returns./analysis: Statistical calculation sheets covering descriptive metrics, F-tests, $\chi^2$ tests, and regression results.UsageThe analysis is encapsulated in a Google Sheets document, which contains automated calculations for risk assessment and statistical significance testing.DisclaimerThis project is for educational purposes only. Past financial performance is not indicative of future results.
